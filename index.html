Theory:

Water that is hard prevents lathering of soap. It is a characteristic water quality parameter. It is caused due to presence of certain salts of calcium, magnesium and other heavy metals dissolved in water. A sample of hard water, when treated with soap forms a white scum or precipitate. This precipitate is formed, due to the formation of insoluble soaps of calcium and magnesium. This water which does not produce lather with soap solution readily, but forms a white scum, is called hard water. Water which lathers easily on shaking with soap solution, is called soft water.

The degree of hardness of drinking water has been classified in terms of the equivalent CaCO3 concentration as follows:
Soft 0-60 mg/L
Medium 60-120mg/L
Hard 120-180mg/L
Very Hard >180mg/L

Sodium salt of Ethylene diamine tetra-acetic acid (EDTA) forms a chelated soluble complex when added to a solution of certain metal cations. If a small amount of a dye such as Eriochrome black T is added to an aqueous solution containing calcium and magnesium ions at a pH of 10 (plus or minus)0.1, the solution will become wine red. If EDTA is then added as a titrant, the calcium and magnesium will be complexed. After sufficient EDTA has been added to complex all the magnesium and calcium, the solution will turn from wine red to blue due to the presence of free indicator. This is the end point of the titration.


Unit of Hardness expressed in a different ways:

1. Parts per million (ppm):is the parts of calcium carbonate equivalent hardness per 106parts of water, i.e, 1 ppm = 1 part of CaCO3 eq hardness in 106parts of water.

2. Milligram per litre (mg/L):is the number of milligrams of CaCO3 equivalent hardness present per litre of water. Thus:1 mg/L = 1 mg of CaCO3 eq hardness per L of water.

 

3. Clarke degree (oCl):is the parts of CaCO3equivalent hardness per 70,000 parts of water. Thus, 1oClarke = 1 grain of CaCO3 eq hardness per gallon of water.

4.Degree French (oFr):Is the parts of CaCO3 equivalent hardness per 105 parts of water. Thus, 1oFr = 1 part of CaCO3hardness eq per 105parts of water.

Relationship Between Various Units of Hardness:

Procedure:

a. Standardization of EDTA

1. Fill the burette with EDTA

2. Pipette out 20 ml of standard hard water into a clean conical flask

3. Add 5 ml of buffer solution and 4 drops of Eriochrome Black –T indicator.

4. The solution turns wine red in colour.

5.Adjust the speed of the drops of EDTA from the burette

6.Titrate the wine red coloured solution against the EDTA.

7. When the color changes from wine red to steel blue, stop the titration and note the volume of EDTA used.

8.This is the end point.

9.Repeat the titration for concordant values. Let the concordant value be V1 ml.
